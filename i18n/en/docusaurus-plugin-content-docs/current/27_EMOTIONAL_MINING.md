## 2. Definition

### 2.1 Formal Definition

**Emotional Mining** — the process of generating verifiable economic value from continuous, cryptographically signed data about a person's physical, emotional, and mental wellbeing.

### 2.2 Process Architecture

```
EMOTIONAL MINING
├── INPUT: Health care behavior
│   ├── Emotional wellbeing (HRV, EDA → 11 emotions)

└── PROOF: Proof of Wellness
    ├── Cryptographic signature (TEE)
    ├── Cross-validation of 11 metrics
```

## 7. Ethical Framework

### 7.1 Principles

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Data sovereignty** | User owns their data |
| **No addiction formation** | Monthly Yield motivates without creating gambling behavior |
| **Inclusivity** | Adaptive thresholds accounting for age, gender, and health status |
| **Transparency** | Open formulas, on-chain proof, public audits |
| **Privacy-first** | Encryption, GDPR, opt-in for any sharing |
| **Healthy competition** | Leaderboards motivate without creating psychological pressure |

### 7.2 Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Obsessive self-monitoring | AI detects anxious patterns and recommends "rest day" |
| Anxiety at low WVI | Positive framing, emphasis on progress, not absolute value |
| Data privacy | GDPR, encryption, right to delete, minimal data collection |
| Emotion manipulation | Independent ethics committee, third-party audit |
